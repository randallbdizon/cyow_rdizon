I've provided some of my favorite music theorists under the Channels tab.


These music theorists cover a wide variety of things within the musical world from general theory, to gig vlogs, to musical history, to odd controversies regarding 432hz vs 440hz.



Adam Neely is a jazz musician who specializes in bass and piano. He delves into a ton of music culture from the past to current (particularly in jazz and pop). He is part of a band called Sungazer and he occasionally posts vlogs about his most current tours, how he deals with hiccups, or what to expect from being a gig musician.

Andrew Huang is a guitarist who delves into music production from the olden analogue days, to digital, to computer software, or even apps on your phone. He also touches on experimentation with sounds, styles, or tech. Currently, he has an online music production class (which can be found in his about tab in his YouTube Channel) and 40+ albums on Spotify, Patreon, and Apple Music.

Charles Cornell is a jazz musician who specializes in piano, but can also play drums, trumpet, and does a bit of vocals. He does a lot of music analyses on genres, movies, shows, video games, and etc., usually jazz-related. He is probably well-known for taking meme videos and making music out of them with little to no pitch shifting on the videos themselves.

Nahre Sol is a classical musician who touches on the modern classical side of things. She often makes videos about making classical versions of famous modern songs in the style of old composers like Beethoven, Mozart, Bach, Debussy, and so on. She also touches on learning different styles of music like boogie woogie, jazz, or bebop, which forces her to get out of her comfort zone as a concert classical pianist.

So, if you're an aspiring musician looking for content to help you along your musical journey, please give their channels a look!
